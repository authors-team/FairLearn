A toolkit to assess and improve the fairness of AI systems.

Fairlearn empowers developers of AI systems to assess their systems' fairness and mitigate observed unfairness.

Together, the components of Fairlearn empower data scientists and business leaders to navigate any trade-offs between fairness and model performance, and to select the mitigation algorithm that best fits their needs.

What Do We Mean by Unfairness?

There are many ways that an AI system can behave unfairly. Fairlearn focuses on negative impacts for groups of people, such as those deﬁned in terms of race, gender, age, or disability status.

Quality of Service

A voice recognition system might fail to work as well for women as it does for men.

Allocation

A system for screening loan or job applications might be much better at picking good candidates among white men than among other groups.

Broader Picture

Fairness is fundamentally a socio-technical challenge. There are many aspects of fairness, such as justice and due process, that are not captured by Fairlearn. Moreover , our fairness criteria cannot all be simultaneously satisﬁed, so our priority is enabling data scientists and business leaders to make trade-oﬀs that best ﬁt their needs.

Use Fairlearn

Fairlearn provides metrics, interactive visualizations, and algorithms that enable data scientists and AI developers to assess existing models, train new models, and ultimately choose the one that is right for their application.

Who should use Fairlearn

Data scientists and AI developers

Practitioners can easily experiment with the selection of glassbox models or greybox/blackbox explainers to find the technique that’s right for their application to generate model explanations. They can also choose interactive visualizations to perform what if analysis and perturbation scenarios, learning about model’s potential unfairness or error and issues.

Model evaluators

InterpretML is designed to make it simple for the research community to add new interpretability techniques comparing it to the state-of-the-art techniques available in the toolkit.

Business leaders

To understand and shape the impact of AI systems on users and other stakeholders To reach the full diversity of users with high-quality service To build trust with users and other stakeholders

Students and researchers

To benchmark mitigation algorithms and contribute new ones

How to Assess Fairness with Fairlearn

Select the fairness metric that best ﬁts your needs

Fairlearn supports a wide range of fairness metrics to evaluate the impact of model predictions on diﬀerent groups of people, covering both classiﬁcation and regression tasks.

  • Classiﬁcation: demographic parity, parity in accuracy rate, worst-case accuracy rate, parity in recall, parity in precision, [see all the supported fairness metrics]
  • Regression: parity in mean squared error , worst-case mean squared error , parity in log loss, worst-case log loss, [see all the supported fairness metrics]

    • Navigate trade-oﬀs between performance and fairness with interactive visualizations

    Fairlearn provides an interactive visualization dashboard to investigate the performance and fairness of a single model and to compare multiple models.

  • See how the performance and fairness metrics vary for diﬀerent sensitive features (e.g., race, gender,age).
  • Select the model whose performance and fairness characteristics best ﬁt your needs.

    • How to Mitigate Unfairness with Fairlearn

    Select the fairness metric

    Fairlearn supports mitigation under several fairness metrics for both classiﬁcation and regression.

  • Classiﬁcation: demographic parity, equalized odds, worst-case accuracy rate
  • Regression: worst-case mean squared error , worst-case log loss

    • Select the mitigation algorithm

    Fairlearn provides mitigation algorithms that can be incorporated into diﬀerent stages of an AI pipeline.

  • Model training—use one of our algorithmic reductions to turn your current training algorithm into an algorithm that optimizes model performance under fairness constraints
  • Model postprocessing—take an existing model and ﬁnd the transformation of its output that optimizes performance under fairness constraints
